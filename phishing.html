Hello,

Our campus is frequently targeted by spam and phishing attempts through emails, phone calls, and text messages. Many of these scams impersonate our UCSC colleagues or pose as job offers, while others mimic trusted services like DocuSign and Google Docs. Some are simply unwanted marketing communications.

Lately, there has been a significant rise in phishing emails aimed at our campus community. Scammers are constantly finding new ways to bypass our defenses by using different, easily created email addresses using free email services, such as Gmail or Yahoo. Other times they are stolen email accounts. We urge everyone in the campus community to stay alert and report any phishing and spam emails to Google.

Important Note:

Sometimes, emails that should be flagged as spam or phishing may still end up in your inbox. It’s crucial to remain cautious with every email you receive. UCSC does not verify incoming emails—we rely on Google’s spam filter, which is not perfect. To protect yourself, always double-check the sender’s email address instead of relying on the display name, especially if the email seems unexpected or suspicious.

Resources

To stay informed, visit The Phish Bowl, which lists the latest phishing scams received by UCSC students, staff, and faculty. You’ll also find guidance on what to do if you receive a phishing email. This resource will be regularly updated with new information.

Please note: If the sender's email address has been reported to the Phish Bowl, it will not be added.
